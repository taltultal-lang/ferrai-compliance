Question of 10

What is your role in relation to this AI system?

Under the EU AI Act, obligations differ based on whether you place the system on the market, deploy it, or use it.

What domain does this AI system operate in?

EU AI Act Annex III defines high-risk categories based on deployment sector.

Does this system make or significantly inform high-stakes decisions?

Examples: decisions affecting credit access, employment, housing, education admission, criminal sentencing, healthcare treatment, or government benefits.

Does this system process biometric data?

Biometric data includes facial features, fingerprints, voice patterns, iris, gait, or other body-based identifiers.

Is this real-time facial recognition in a publicly accessible space?

This is one of the key Prohibited AI practices under EU AI Act Art. 5(1)(h), with very limited law enforcement exceptions.

Does this system use subliminal techniques or exploit psychological vulnerabilities?

Under EU AI Act Art. 5, systems designed to manipulate people below the threshold of consciousness, or to exploit age, disability, or social/economic situations are Prohibited.

Does this system evaluate or classify individuals for social trustworthiness?

Social scoring by public authorities or in a way that leads to detrimental treatment in unrelated contexts is Prohibited under EU AI Act Art. 5(1)(c).

Is this system built on or integrated with a General-Purpose AI (GPAI) model? GPAI models (like GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) are trained on broad data for multiple tasks. Under EU AI Act Title X, GPAI model providers face specific transparency and safety obligations, including for "systemic risk" models above 10^25 FLOPs.

Examples: using OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Mistral, or similar foundation model APIs as the intelligence layer.
